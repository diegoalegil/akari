# Akari — DESIGN.md

> The design prompt library for Akari (灯). Each line is a concrete, implementable
> directive to push the UI toward a breathtaking, premium, "anime-but-refined" result.
> **193 directives** across 23 sections + signature set.
> Dark by default · lantern soul · two hero motion moments (card-flip, kanji stroke-draw)
> · anime humor only in chrome/AI, never in validated content · respects prefers-reduced-motion.

## North star

Akari is a dark room with a single lantern hanging just above the top edge of the screen — and every screen you open is the act of that lantern catching light. The whole app lives on warm-tinted ink (never pure black), and warmth means exactly one thing everywhere: brand, ambiance, reward — a soft sakura-into-ember glow that blooms but never neons (capped ~16%). Cool indigo means exactly one other thing: this is where your attention and your keyboard are. The kanji are not text, they are display objects lit from within, treated with the reverence of a museum placard, and the two moments the app is allowed to feel cinematic are the card turning (220ms, a turn-and-settle, glow brightening at the apex) and the brush drawing a character stroke by stroke with an ember tip lifting off the page. Motion is short, soft, and earned — under 220ms outside those two heroes, every transform interruptible, every flourish gone under prefers-reduced-motion while the meaningful glow stays. The Japanese always breathes looser than the Latin around it. Anime warmth is real but rationed — it lives only in Spanish UI chrome (the filler-episode rest day, the 'Continuará…' end-card, the Sensei voice) and stops dead at a hairline before any validated reading, gloss, or sentence. Opening Akari should feel, every single time, like striking a match in a quiet, beautifully art-directed dark — present, premium, and never once shouting.

## ★ Signature directives (cross-cutting)

1. ONE LIGHT SOURCE, GLOBALLY WIRED: drive every warm glow in the app — logo flame, focal-card bloom, CTA shadow, streak lantern, heatmap peak, completion bloom — from a single `--akari-glow` custom property and a shared two-layer radial `--glow-warm` token (inner #F2B66B 14%, outer #FF7E9D 6%), so the entire product reads as lit by one lantern hanging high-center above the viewport; hard-cap the brightest single stop at 16% app-wide and forbid any component from authoring its own warm shadow off-token.
2. TWO NON-OVERLAPPING LIGHT LANGUAGES, ENFORCED AS LAW: warm ember/sakura is ALWAYS brand/ambient/reward and cool indigo-night (#6C7BFF) is ALWAYS focus/active/keyboard/AI — they may never trade roles. Every `:focus-visible` is the same 2px indigo ring + 4px rgba(108,123,255,0.18) halo with a 2px ink offset, rendered as box-shadow (never outline, never layout shift); if a glow is warm it means something good happened, if it's cool it means 'you are here.'
3. NO PURE BLACK, NO PURE WHITE, NO SOLID LINES — globally: darkest pixel #0B0C0F, lightest text the warm off-white #F4F1EC at 0.72 body / 0.48 meta, every divider an alpha-white hairline (rgba(255,255,255,0.06)), every surface elevation built from light (inset top highlight + downward ambient shadow) rather than borders; the app shell carries a single fixed top-to-bottom #16181F→#0E0F13 ramp so every screen shares 'light comes from up here.'
4. GLOW IS A VERB, NOT DECORATION: ambient chrome sits at a barely-there 4–6% and NEVER animates its glow; warm light only brightens on a genuine event (correct Good/Easy ramps 8%→14% and back over 180ms, a streak milestone gets one 600ms pulse, a completed session 'ignites' the lantern). Brightening must always mean something just happened — ban purely decorative glow animation.
5. THE GLOBAL MOTION CONTRACT: durations come only from `--motion-fast 120ms / --motion-base 180ms / --motion-slow 220ms` and easings only from `--ease-akari` cubic-bezier(0.22,1,0.36,1) (enter) and `--ease-akari-out` cubic-bezier(0.4,0,0.2,1) (exit); nothing exceeds 220ms outside the two heroes, no hardcoded magic numbers in any framer-motion transition, stagger caps at ~0.28s regardless of count, and every animation is interruptible — re-triggering redirects from the current value, never queues.
6. PROTECT THE TWO HEROES BY STARVING EVERYTHING ELSE: the card-flip and the kanji stroke-draw are the only moments allowed to feel cinematic, so route transitions, list entrances, and chrome must stay deliberately quiet (soft fades, tight directional staggers, no bounces, no count-up tickers on SRS data). Scarcity is the strategy — if every surface sparkles, the heroes stop landing.
7. ONE HONEST REDUCED-MOTION PATH, NOT A KILL SWITCH: a single `useReducedMotion()` guard replaces the flip with a ~120ms cross-fade, renders strokes instantly fully-drawn, drops all translate/scale/stagger/particles — but KEEPS the meaningful glow state-changes at reduced intensity because they carry meaning, and proves the splash, empty states, and stats all degrade to calm static brand moments rather than dead snaps.
8. THE VALIDATION FIREWALL, MADE STRUCTURAL: all anime-flavored Spanish lives in one typed `microcopy.ts` catalog physically separated from learning-content strings, with a banned-register list (no SCREAMING CAPS, no UwU/senpai/baka, no emoji-spam, ≤6 words per toast, one anime reference max); the AI 'Explícame/Sensei' surface is ALWAYS fenced — indigo dotted border, indigo-tinted glass, pinned 'IA · puede fallar' disclaimer, Inter commentary voice never the editorial kanji treatment — so a flavor line or a generated claim can never be mistaken for canonical reading/gloss/example.
9. KANJI ARE DISPLAY TYPOGRAPHY EVERYWHERE, LIT FROM WITHIN: any headword glyph renders in --font-jp at an editorial clamp scale (≥3.5rem), weight 500, with exactly one decoration — a single soft warm halo anchored at its optical center that lifts ~8%→16% during a reveal/draw then settles; Japanese always gets looser line-height (1.9 JP vs Latin), furigana is real `<ruby>` capped at half the base glyph, and the glyph never reflows or font-swaps mid-animation (pre-measured, size-adjusted fallbacks).
10. SCREEN-READER & ZOOM PARITY FOR JAPANESE AS A FIRST-CLASS PROMISE: every JP string carries `lang="ja"` plus an `aria-label` of reading + Spanish gloss (水 — みず — agua), furigana `<rt>` is `aria-hidden` to stop double-reads, the animated stroke SVG is `role="img"` with a visually-hidden ordered stroke-direction list, the full SRS loop is keyboard-only (Space flip, 1–4 grade, J audio, R replay, ? cheat-sheet), and the entire type scale is rem-based clamp() so 200% zoom never clips the review card — premium that excludes no one.
11. FOCUS-DIM AS THE APP'S BREATH: entering a study session dims all chrome (rail, stats) to ~35% opacity + pointer-events:none over 200ms and restores it on session-complete, so starting and finishing review literally feels like house lights dimming and rising and the learner is left alone with the lit card; pair this with the persistent 2px ember→sakura progress filament so momentum reads as a quietly advancing line of light, never a number.
12. SOUND IS ONE FELT MALLET ON WARM WOOD, DEFAULT OFF: if audio is ever added it ships as a single tuned soft-pentatonic palette through a gentle ADSR + lowpass (never the bright 'app ping'), grade results are pitched chords mapped to the muted semantic family, the card-flip gets a 90–140ms paper rustle synced to the flip's midpoint, the stroke-draw stays reverently silent, and only session-complete earns one low ~400ms 'exhale' tone — the single moment the app is allowed to sing.

## Brand & Logo `#brand-logo`

1. Build the isotype as a single inline SVG of 灯 set in a heavy display weight, but redraw the right-side radical (丁) so its vertical stroke reads as a hanging lantern cord and the fire radical (火) on the left becomes the flame: render the flame strokes with a `fill` of `url(#akari-ember)`, a vertical linearGradient from ember #F2B66B (top) to sakura #FF7E9D (bottom), while the cord/frame strokes stay ink #E7E9F0 at 92% opacity — this two-material split (warm glow body, cool structural frame) is the entire identity and must survive down to 16px.
2. The wordmark locks the isotype 灯 to the left of 'AKARI' set in Geist/Inter at 600 weight, tracked +0.14em uppercase, with the baseline of the Latin optically centered to the lantern's flame core (not its bounding box); reserve clear-space equal to the cap-height of 'A' on all sides and never let any UI element, glow, or border intrude into it.
3. Give the flame a perpetual 'breathing-glow': wrap the flame `<path>` in a framer-motion element animating `filter: drop-shadow(0 0 6px rgba(242,182,107,.45))` → `drop-shadow(0 0 16px rgba(255,126,157,.6))` and `scale` 1 → 1.035 over a 3.2s `easeInOut` yoyo loop, transform-origin at the flame base so it flickers upward like a real lantern rather than pulsing symmetrically.
4. On true first load only (gate with a `localStorage` flag), play the 'ignite' sequence: the lantern frame strokes draw on via SVG `stroke-dashoffset` from full to 0 over 520ms `easeOut`, then at 420ms the flame fades up from `opacity:0; scale:.6` to full with a single 90ms warm-white bloom overshoot on the drop-shadow, then settles directly into the breathing loop — total under 1.1s, and the whole thing is skipped (mark shown lit and breathing) for the second-visit-onward and for `prefers-reduced-motion`.
5. Define one CSS custom property `--akari-glow` on `:root` driving every flame shadow so the mark, the focus ring, and product glow accents share a single tunable light source; in light contexts (rare) swap to `--akari-glow-ink` and let the flame gradient darken its ember stop to #D99A4A so the mark never blooms into illegibility on pale surfaces.
6. Ship a strict mono variant: the exact same 灯 geometry rendered single-color via `fill: currentColor` with NO gradient and NO glow, for places that demand restraint — disabled states, print, embedded email, the OS share sheet, and any context smaller than 14px where the two-tone split muddies; this variant must be a separate `<symbol>` reference, never the color mark with effects stripped at runtime.
7. Generate the favicon and PWA/maskable icon from the isotype alone (never the wordmark): center the lantern at 66% of the safe zone on a #0E0F13 rounded-square, bake a faint static radial ember glow behind the flame directly into the raster so even a 32px tab favicon carries the warm-lantern signature, and provide the 512px maskable with 20% padding so Android's mask never clips the flame.
8. Establish three responsive lockups switched by container width, not viewport: full wordmark (isotype + 'AKARI') at ≥120px, isotype-only for the collapsed sidebar / mobile nav at 28–40px, and the mono glyph for inline/16px — wire these as a single `<Logo variant>` React component so the breathing-glow attaches only at ≥28px and is omitted below to protect performance and legibility.
9. Place the lit, breathing wordmark exactly once per screen as the quiet anchor — top-left of the app shell at 24px isotype height — and forbid it from competing with content: it must never animate on route changes, never appear inside cards, and its glow radius must stay below the elevation glow of any active primary button so the brand feels present but never shouts over the learning surface.
10. For the loading/splash and session-handoff moments, let the breathing lantern BE the spinner: center the isotype at 48px on the ink field with the flame's breathing loop tightened to 1.4s and a faint 1px sakura→ember conic-gradient arc sweeping the lantern frame once per cycle as a progress hint — replacing any generic spinner, so even waiting feels like watching a lantern hold its light.

## Intro / Splash `#intro-splash`

1. LANTERN IGNITION as the hero beat: render the lantern as a single SVG paper-shade silhouette on the ink backdrop (#0E0F13), and at t=0 animate a radial-gradient glow core from scale 0.6/opacity 0 to scale 1/opacity 1 over 420ms with ease-out [0.22,1,0.36,1], its color sweeping warm ember (#F2B66B) at the core into sakura pink (#FF7E9D) at the falloff edge — the moment must read as warmth flooding INTO the paper, not a lamp switching on, so the glow's box-shadow/filter blur radius should grow from 8px to ~64px as it ignites.
2. STAGGER THE WICK before the flood: precede the full ignition with a 90ms ember 'catch' — a tiny 6px ember-colored point at the lantern's base flickers up in opacity 0→1→0.7 (two keyframes, 60ms+30ms) before the radial glow expands, giving the ignition a physical cause; gate the flicker behind prefers-reduced-motion (reduced users get the glow at full opacity instantly with no flicker).
3. KANA-TO-ROMAJI REVEAL as editorial display type: center 'アカリ' in Zen Kaku Gothic New at a confident ~72px, each of the 3 kana fading+rising (y:12px→0, opacity 0→1) on a 70ms stagger starting at 380ms (overlapping the glow's tail), then at 820ms cross-dissolve the kana to romaji 'AKARI' (Geist, tracked +0.12em, 28px) — the kana scales down to 0.82 and drops opacity to 0 while romaji rises from opacity 0/y:6px over 200ms, so the name 'resolves' from Japanese into Latin like focus snapping in.
4. GLOW-LIT TYPOGRAPHY, not flat white: the kana/romaji must inherit the lantern's light — fill them with a warm off-white (#F7F0E8) and apply a text-shadow tinted ember (0 0 24px rgba(242,182,107,0.35)) whose intensity is driven by the same animation clock as the glow core, so the letters appear illuminated BY the lantern rather than drawn on top of it; the shadow settles to a faint 0 0 12px resting glow.
5. SETTLING LIGHT PARTICLES as the denouement: spawn 14–18 sub-2px particles in ember/sakura from the glow core at t=600ms, drifting outward+upward with randomized velocity and a 700ms ease-out, each fading 1→0 and shrinking to 0 — use a single framer-motion list with per-particle custom delays (max stagger 120ms) rather than a canvas loop, keep total particle motion under the 1.2s envelope, and render zero particles under prefers-reduced-motion.
6. ALWAYS-SKIPPABLE, NEVER-BLOCKING contract: mount the splash as a fixed overlay with pointer-events that never trap focus — the underlying app is interactive from frame one underneath; show a low-key 'Saltar' affordance bottom-right in indigo-night (#6C7BFF) at 60% opacity (appears at 300ms, full opacity on hover), and bind both click and any keydown/pointerdown anywhere to immediately run the exit; the splash must self-dismiss at ~1.15s regardless.
7. GRACEFUL EXIT, not a hard cut: on completion or skip, fade the entire overlay opacity 1→0 over 220ms with ease-in while letting the lantern glow contract scale 1→0.94, and on skip specifically fast-forward (don't snap) — clamp remaining animations to a 180ms tail so an early skip still feels like the light 'pulls away' rather than vanishing, then unmount and set the session flag.
8. ONCE-PER-SESSION + reduced-motion as first-class paths: gate the whole sequence behind a sessionStorage 'akari:splash-seen' flag set on exit so reloads skip it silently; and ship a complete prefers-reduced-motion variant — no flicker, no particles, no rising kana — that simply cross-fades the resting lantern glow + 'AKARI' romaji in over a single 200ms opacity tween and auto-dismisses at 600ms, proving the splash degrades to a calm static brand moment.

## Color & Light `#color-light`

1. Treat 'akari' as a real radial light source, not a flat glow: define a reusable `--glow-warm` token built from a 2-layer radial-gradient (inner #F2B66B at 14% opacity, outer #FF7E9D at 6%, fading to transparent by 60% radius) and render it as a non-interactive ::before sized 1.6x its host, so the lantern light reads as ambient bloom around the focal element (active SRS card, primary CTA) rather than a hard-edged ring; cap the brightest single glow stop at 16% opacity app-wide so it never tips into neon.
2. Build elevation from light, not borders: surfaces step from #0E0F13 (base canvas) to #16181F (raised card) to #1B1E27 (popover/modal), and on every raised surface stack a 1px inner top highlight (`box-shadow: inset 0 1px 0 rgba(255,255,255,0.05)`) plus a soft downward ambient shadow (`0 8px 30px rgba(0,0,0,0.45)`) — the top edge catches light, the body casts shadow, so cards feel physically lifted off the ink instead of outlined by a stroke.
3. Reserve indigo-night #6C7BFF exclusively for focus and active-state light: focus rings render as a 2px #6C7BFF outline plus a diffused `0 0 0 4px rgba(108,123,255,0.18)` halo (never a sakura/ember glow), so warm light always means 'brand/ambient/reward' and cool indigo light always means 'this is where your attention/keyboard is' — two non-overlapping light languages the eye learns instantly.
4. Render the kanji-as-display moment with a directional 'lit-from-within' treatment: the large editorial glyph sits in near-white #F4F1EC while a single tight `--glow-warm` bloom is anchored behind its optical center at ~10% opacity, and the stroke-draw reveal briefly lifts that bloom to 16% over the 220ms draw then settles back to 8% — the character literally warms as it's written, the premium hero beat, and the whole pulse is suppressed under prefers-reduced-motion to a static 8%.
5. Keep semantic grade colors muted and equal-weight by deriving them as low-chroma tones over ink rather than pure hues — Again #C76B6B, Hard #D9A05B, Good #6FB08A, Easy #6FB6C2 — each used at ~85% as text/icon and pulled to 12–16% alpha for its fill/track, so the four answer buttons read as a calm tonal family (no single saturated 'danger red' shouting), with grade meaning carried by hue + position, never by raw intensity.
6. Let glow respond to state with restraint, never decoration: the warm lantern bloom intensifies only on genuine reward moments — a correct 'Easy/Good' answer ramps the card's `--glow-warm` from 8% to 14% and back over 180ms with soft ease-out, a streak milestone gives one slow 600ms warm pulse — while neutral/idle UI carries glow at a barely-there 4–6%; ambient chrome never animates its glow, so brightening always *means* something happened.
7. Establish dark-theme depth with a vertical light gradient on the app shell: the root background is a subtle top-to-bottom ramp from #16181F at the very top to #0E0F13 at the fold (as if the lantern hangs above the viewport), and pin one extremely faint fixed `--glow-warm` wash (max 5% opacity) high-center behind all content so every screen shares a single consistent 'light comes from up here' source rather than reading as flat #000-ish black.
8. Forbid pure black and pure white anywhere: the darkest pixel is #0B0C0F and the lightest text is #F4F1EC (a warm off-white, never #FFFFFF), body text sits at rgba(244,241,236,0.72) and secondary/meta at 0.48, and all dividers are alpha-white hairlines (`rgba(255,255,255,0.06)`) rather than solid grey lines — the warm-tinted neutrals and translucent strokes keep the ink surfaces feeling lit and atmospheric instead of clinical and cold.

Establish a strict three-tier answer hierarchy by role, not just size: READING (kana) sits in --font-jp at 1.5rem/500 in --color-ember; MEANING (Spanish gloss) in --font-sans at 1.125rem/600 in --color-fg; and the part-of-speech/tag in --font-sans 0.75rem/600 uppercase tracking-[0.14em] in --color-fg-faint — the ember reading and the bright gloss must never share a weight or a color so the eye ranks them instantly.

Cap learning-content reading measure at 38–42 Japanese characters / 60–66 Latin characters via max-width on the example-sentence container (roughly 34ch for JP set in --font-jp, 62ch for the EN translation in --font-sans), and set example sentences in --font-jp at 1.375rem with line-height 1.9 to give kana+kanji the vertical air they need — Japanese must always breathe looser than the Latin around it.

Lock a modular type scale on a 1.25 ratio exposed as CSS custom props (--text-xs .75 / --text-sm .875 / --text-base 1 / --text-lg 1.25 / --text-xl 1.5625 / --text-2xl 1.953 / --display clamp) and pair each step with an intentional line-height (tight 1.1 for display, 1.5 for UI, 1.9 for JP body); ban arbitrary one-off font-size values in components so rhythm stays consistent across kana trainer, SRS, and the Explícame panel.
8. Differentiate the AI 'Explícame' persona typographically from validated content: set its prose in --font-sans 0.9375rem/1.7 --color-fg-muted with a 2px --color-indigo-night left rule and a small indigo 'AI' label in 0.6875rem uppercase tracking-[0.16em], while any Japanese it cites stays in --font-jp at --color-fg — so the reader can always see at a glance where generated explanation ends and the canonical reading/gloss begins.

## Motion Principles `#motion-principles`

1. Define a single global motion scale as CSS variables and reuse it everywhere: durations --motion-fast 120ms (taps, toggles, hovers), --motion-base 180ms (most enter/exit, panel slides), --motion-slow 220ms (card-flip, large surfaces), plus two signature easings --ease-akari cubic-bezier(0.22,1,0.36,1) for entrances and --ease-akari-out cubic-bezier(0.4,0,0.2,1) for exits; ban any framer-motion `transition` that hardcodes a number not drawn from this scale, and never exceed 220ms outside the two hero moments.
2. Treat the lantern glow as a motion primitive, not decoration: on any focus/active state (review answer reveal, kana-trainer correct, primary CTA), animate a radial box-shadow from `0 0 0 0 transparent` to a soft `0 0 40px -8px` in sakura (#FF7E9D) or ember (#F2B66B) over --motion-base with --ease-akari, holding at ~35% opacity then easing back so the surface feels like it 'breathes light' rather than blinks.
3. Choreograph list and grid entrances with a tight directional stagger using framer-motion variants: parent `staggerChildren: 0.035` (cap total at ~0.28s regardless of count), each child rising 8px with opacity 0→1 on --ease-akari; reserve stagger strictly for first-mount and route transitions, and switch to AnimatePresence `popLayout` for in-place add/remove so reordering never re-triggers the whole cascade.
4. Hero moment one, the card-flip: drive it with framer-motion `rotateY` 0→180 over --motion-slow (220ms) on --ease-akari with `transformStyle: preserve-3d` and a parent `perspective: 1200px`, fading the front face out and back face in across the 40–60% midpoint so the kanji never appears mirrored, and add a 1–2px scale dip (1→0.98→1) at the flip's apex to give the card a subtle physical 'settle'.
5. Hero moment two, the stroke-draw: animate each SVG stroke path with `pathLength` 0→1 via framer-motion, sequencing strokes with a per-stroke delay of ~90ms and an individual draw duration of 380–520ms scaled to path length, on --ease-akari-out so each stroke decelerates into its endpoint like a brush lifting; lead the drawing tip with a faint ember glow (animated `filter: drop-shadow`) and never animate all strokes simultaneously.
6. Use shared-element transitions for genuine spatial continuity only: give a card and its expanded detail/study view the same framer-motion `layoutId` so the kanji morphs in place over --motion-slow, and restrict `layout`/`layoutId` to these one or two intentional promotions per screen — never apply it to list items in flux, which causes layout-thrash jank.
7. Author one honest `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` fallback path, not a kill switch: replace the card-flip with a 120ms cross-fade between faces, render the stroke-draw as an instant full-opacity reveal with an optional 1s static ghost of completed strokes, drop all translate/scale/stagger to simple opacity fades, but KEEP the glow state-changes (they convey meaning, not flourish) at reduced intensity.
8. Codify when NOT to animate: text content, numbers, and SRS grade labels change instantly (no count-up tickers, no sliding digits); never animate scroll position, never auto-play the stroke-draw or glow on mount without an explicit user trigger (tap/reveal/hover), and ensure any in-flight animation is interruptible — re-triggering an action must redirect from the current value, never queue or block input.

## Layout & Navigation `#layout-nav`

Build the shell as a fixed 72px left icon-rail (not a full sidebar) on a slightly-recessed surface (#0E0F13 against the #16181F content plane, separated only by a 1px inset border at rgba(255,255,255,0.04) plus a soft inner shadow) holding 5 items max — Hoy (session), Mazo, Kana, Estadísticas, Ajustes — each a 40px rounded-xl target; the active item gets a 2px-wide vertical ember-to-sakura gradient bar flush on the rail's left edge plus a 12px radial glow bleeding from behind the icon, and the indicator slides between items with framer-motion layoutId on a 180ms [0.22,1,0.36,1] ease so navigation feels like a lantern being carried, not a tab snapping.

Make grade feedback a pitched chord, not a generic 'correct' beep: map the four SRS grades to the muted semantic palette tonally — Again is a single low muted note with a touch of detune (felt, not buzzer-harsh), Hard a flat two-note, Good a clean rising perfect-fifth, Easy a soft major-third-up arpeggio with a faint shimmer tail — each ~140–200ms, peaking at -18dBFS, so the ear learns the result before the eyes read the color.
3. Bind the card flip to a single short paper/cloth rustle (a 90–140ms filtered-noise burst, bandpassed ~800Hz–2.5kHz with a fast exponential decay, panned center) that fires at the exact frame the flip animation crosses 90deg — sync it to the framer-motion onUpdate/midpoint, not the click, so the sound feels like it's caused by the page turning rather than the tap, and the kanji stroke-draw gets no audio at all to keep that moment reverent and visual.
4. Default sound OFF and gate it behind a single 'Sonido' toggle in settings that, the first time it's enabled, immediately plays one preview 'Good' chime so the user calibrates the volume in their head before a session — persist the state in localStorage, hard-mute when prefers-reduced-motion is set or the tab is hidden (visibilitychange), and never play two cues within 60ms (debounce) so rapid grading can't stack into a gamey rattle.
5. Engineer restraint as a feature: keep master output ceiling at -14dBFS, add +/-12 cents of random pitch micro-variation per trigger so repeated cues never sound like a copy-paste loop, route everything through one shared GainNode with a 40ms fade-in on first unlock to avoid the click/pop of a cold AudioContext, and reserve a slightly longer, lower 'breath' resolve tone (a single ~400ms soft note) exclusively for session-complete — so finishing a deck feels like a quiet exhale, the one moment the app is allowed to sing.
